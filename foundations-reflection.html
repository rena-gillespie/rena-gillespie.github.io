Foundations Reflections

Human Skills

  • What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning

    • What Ive learnt about me and my ego is it is easily inflatable and easily humbled. I get very happy and proud of myself
    for completing a task, maybe get a bit carried away and when I hit a block I hit it hard.

  • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

    • It's good to know what other people’s values are and what's important to them, it gives you a good idea of what kind of
    person you’re working with and who they’re working with.

    Empathy is a skill that is learned and helps with relationships with other people and to understand each other in a team.

    Self awareness is valuable to understand your own limits, how you may appear to others. While it could be difficult for
    some people to balance self awareness to self consciousness, it’s always important to be respectful to the people around
    you and the impact you can make to them.

  • What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

    • What surprised me is how much more self aware I’ve become while learning Human Skills, while learning neuroplasticity
    I started being a bit more conscious of my own habits and what is making my brain more rigid or flexible.

  • What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

    • A challenging part was writing something personal and required a bit of insight for ourselves, or doing the listening
    and looping challenge. It'd take a lot of time to muster up the courage to ask someone to sit in a voice channel, especially
    someone I'm not familiar with.

  • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

    • I think a programming school dedicating time to teach us human skills is to prepare us for working with classmates,
    clients and workers after we graduate.

  • Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

    • I don’t believe it was a waste of time, not only was it a break from coding, we got to learn about ourselves and what’s
    important to us and it was kind of an icebreaker for our classmates. It was also important to learn about how to take care
    of our wellbeing and keep track of it through journaling. If we just worked on coding I’d imagine there’d be students that would have trouble
    interacting with other students during bootcamp or overwork themselves and lead to burnout.
